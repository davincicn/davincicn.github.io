Translator guidelines

Procedure for submitting new contributions

1) Translator to translate in Crowdin
2) Notify the moderators about the translation and the word count via team channel
3) One of the moderators will proofread the translation
4) The moderator will contact the translator, requesting changes if needed
5) Translator to submit the post
6) Moderator will score the contribution and leave the comment (Now you can go back to step 1)
7) Utopian bot upvotes the post

General guidelines

  • Contributions should be submitted from: https://davinci.utopian.io/ (Other frontend is allowed if you are sure you set correct beneficiaries!)
  • Contributions in this category include original translations created or updated for an active Open Source project.
  • Submission volume should be at least 250 words and should be structured following the template.
  • Translation of static texts (that should not be translated) like links, code and paths, will lead to a significantly lower score of a contribution, thus reducing its chances of receiving a reward.
  • Duplicated strings / texts cannot be considered for the count of words that will impact the score.
  • If a contributor’s lack of proficiency in the source or target language of the translation is apparent, the submission will not be considered for potential reward, and the user may lose their approval to submit contributions to the category or Utopian.io in whole.
  • Translations submitted on CrowdIn should be proofread. – Submissions must include extensive details to allow for the proofing of the translation, and list the tools used to manage the translation work (when applicable).
  • Translations of the same text made by different contributors will be accepted only if the moderator can recognise the higher quality of the newest translation compared to that ones previously submitted.
  • Translations of obviously low quality and machine-generated translations will not be considered for potential reward and the submitting user may be banned.

Dos and Dont’s

Dos

  • Submit via: https://davinci.utopian.io/ (Or other Frontend, e.g. Steempeak as long as you set correct beneficiaries!)
  • Leave comments if you find typos or mistakes in source strings.
  • Contact project owners to clarify things that are not clear.
  • Discuss with your team members if you are not sure with some translations.
  • Check your translations before submitting.

Dont’s

  • Use machine translations - it’s no point to reward you if the strings are translated by Google/Baidu etc.
  • Rush on translations - Utopian gives you very high upvotes and it deserves top quality translations.
  • Rely on language manager to pick up your typos and obvious mistakes, it’s your work and you need to check before submitting.
  • Submit more than 3 posts per week.
  • Start a new translation before your old translations are approved - it will make proofreader’s life harder.

Post Template

(To be completed by the Translator on the page https://davinci.utopian.io/ )

Project Details

(written in your own words, don’t copy & paste)

Describe the target project of your work, including your motivation to translate this particular projects, and what value it brings to the open source community.

Contribution Specifications

Translation Overview Explain the purpose of the text translated, their applications and uses and include an overview of the work you’ve done.

Languages

List the source and target languages in this contribution. Be sure to also provide some insight on your expertise and translation experience in these languages.

Word Count

Present a number report of the volume of the work done (number of words translated). Be sure to exclude duplicate strings and static text (like links and codes) from your count.

Proof of Authorship

Please provide all the necessary screenshots, links & other information related to Crowdin and/or your translation work to verify the authorship of those translations.

Projects
